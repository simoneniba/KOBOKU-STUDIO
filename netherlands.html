01 / Statement Why second hand?

In a country where the canals are older than most countries, the objects that survive have been chosen twice.

The Netherlands treats its second-hand shops with the same seriousness it treats its museums. Kringloopwinkels on every canal, vintage archives in former factories, tailors who restore more than they sell.

With Koboku opening its Utrecht studio in 2026, we're documenting the shops that define Dutch resale culture — and applying our AI creative direction to show their objects the way they deserve to be seen.

02 / Feature
Feature No. 01

De
Kringloper

Utrecht, Vredenburg 40

A multi-floor kringloopwinkel occupying a converted textile warehouse. Where most second-hand stores merchandise chaos, De Kringloper curates: objects are arranged by material, color, and era.

The upper floor holds their archive: pieces pulled aside for their condition, provenance, or sheer strangeness. It is, quietly, one of the best edits of twentieth-century Dutch design in the country.

Est.1998
